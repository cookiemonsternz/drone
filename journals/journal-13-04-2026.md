### Power chain protection

I found a super handy guide on the discord:
![Power guide](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019d8431-13c6-755a-b7b8-45e3f2ea2327/paste-1776039299553.png)

And here is my modified version for the setup I'm using:
![My power setup](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019d8438-e0d7-704c-ba78-3c5960ab16dd/paste-1776039810806.png)
The reason for the 4.5V is because of the diodes voltage drop. 

Pretty easy setup in the schematic, just a tiny bit of swapping out symbols (kicad doesn't have a 4.5v power symbol grrrr).

![Power section](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019d8443-1a9a-7be8-ab2b-a8cc761d2d6b/paste-1776040480940.png)

Another little thing I want is reverse polarity protection for the battery. The other design I'm using as reference also had a fuse, which would be a mightily worthy addition.
The zener diode from the reference is mostly unnecessary for me, as my Vgs will never get above ~8V due to me using 2s batteries.

![Reverse Polarity Protection](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019d8453-899c-7d3e-a8be-d906ef670fc1/paste-1776041558611.png)

The final thing to do with the power section (pretty much) is to swap out the 5v ldo with a dc-dc buck converter. All peripherals run off 5v and it uses a lot of current and LDO's generate a lot of heat. Buck converters are just way more efficient and thus are reccomended for this. I might also have another one seperate for the vtx as with rf stuff it could cause a bunch of noise on things that don't want it.
