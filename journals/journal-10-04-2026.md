Starting to figure out voltage regulator:
- MCU says ~100mA from datasheet
- IMU will probs be <30mA (datasheet says 3-6 but i'm not sure if I read the right parameter lol)
- Camera doesn't say but I'll say ~150mA as a guess
- OSD says max ~150mA 
- ELRS receiver will probs be ~100mA
- Buzzer will be ~50-100mA
- TX5823 is max ~350mA

This gives a total of ~1A, and just to be safe I'll go for 2A.
I quickly foundan LDO which seems decent for the task:
[BL BL8072CLTR50](https://www.lcsc.com/product-detail/C692268.html)
It's got a dropout voltage of ~1.0V at 5V output at 2.0A, which just means my battery voltage can't go below 6V, which is perfect, as this is the voltage of a fully discharged 2S battery. It does mean that I won't be able to use 1S batteries, but I think that was probably already the case from the ESC, except I didn't check lol.

I spent quite a while trying to figure out what resistors I needed before realizing the chip was a fixed output voltage one lol. Anyways, I'm going to start connecting stuff up and report back soon!

---

Sorting out the vtx now, and the chip I'm using just has a dip switch method for channel selection. Most VTX's connect to betaflight and allow configuration through that, but for the sake of simplicity, I'm going to go with a manually configured one, completely bypassing any betaflight things and just taking direct camera signal and transmitting it.

The chip has 3 bit channel selection, so 8 channels total, which are listed in the [datasheet](https://www.scribd.com/document/720542539/TX5823-Spec-V1).

It doesn't have any output power seleciton, but just chucking a resistor before the antenna should work fine, maybe with a switch to select output power and stuff.
